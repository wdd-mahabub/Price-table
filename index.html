    What’s the difference between Registered office address and Director’s Service Address?

    The Registered Office Address is your company’s official address for receiving legal and government Mail.

    The Director’s Service Address is the Director’s official address used for personal correspondence from Companies House or HMRC.

    The same address can be used for both Registered address and directors address. This is the most common practice.

    Our Business address service allows you to use our address on your website, business cards, stationery, marketing materials, and all other business correspondence. Almost everywhere except Companies House and HMRC.

    How are my letters handled?
    Each time we receive a letter addressed to you, we’ll forward it and deduct one credit from your account. One letter = one credit.

    How does the credit system work?
    When purchasing this service, you’ll be asked to add additional credits to your account. You can choose from 10, 20, 30, 40, or 50 credits. The number of credits you choose, is the number of business letters we’ll forward. For example, if you buy 10 credits, we will forward up to 10 letters to you.

    How much do credits cost?
    To see credit prices, simply visit our registration page, select the Business Address Service, and you’ll see the available credit options along with their pricing.

    Will a credit be used for junk mail?
    No — we do not deduct credits for obvious junk mail. We do our best to filter out and discard unwanted or clearly non-business-related items before sending your letters.

    What happens when I run out of credits?
    You’ll receive an email notification when your credits are low or used up. To continue receiving forwarded letters, simply log in and top up your credits at any time.

    Can I carry unused credits into the next year?
    Unused credits are valid for 12 months from the date of purchase. After one year, any unused credits will expire.
